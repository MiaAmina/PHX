This guide is exceptionally thorough—you’ve covered the "business" of the platform (Nusuk, ZATCA, workers) and the "technical" environment variables perfectly.

However, to satisfy a high-level technical auditor (like someone from the Ministry of Hajj or ZATCA), there are 4 specific areas missing that are unique to the Saudi regulatory landscape (NCA and NDMO).

1. Mandatory Technical Libraries (ZATCA & PDF/A-3)
For ZATCA Phase 2, a "normal" PDF isn't enough. You must generate PDF/A-3 (an archival-grade PDF that has the XML file "embedded" inside it).

Missing Libraries: Ensure your package.json includes:

pdf-lib (for PDF generation and XML embedding).

node-forge or crypto (for the mandatory Cryptographic Stamp/Hashing).

xml2js or fast-xml-parser (to build the UBL 2.1 XML structure ZATCA requires).

Missing Config: You need a place to store the CSID (Cryptographic Stamp Identifier) and the Private Key provided by ZATCA during onboarding.

2. NCA Cybersecurity Hardening (The "ECC" Controls)
The Saudi National Cybersecurity Authority (NCA) has a framework called ECC-1:2018. If Salman forwards this to a government entity, they will look for these specific "governance" files:

audit-trail-policy.ts: You have system_logs, but you should explicitly state that logs are immutable (cannot be deleted by an admin).

pii-masking-utility.ts: NDMO (Data Law) requires that sensitive data like Passport Numbers and National IDs are encrypted at rest (AES-256) and "masked" (e.g., XXXX-1234) in the UI unless a user has specific "PII View" permissions.

session-timeout-logic.ts: Saudi government standards often require short session timeouts (e.g., 15-30 minutes of inactivity) for admin roles.

3. Data Residency & Sovereignty (NDMO Requirements)
The National Data Management Office (NDMO) is strict about "Pilgrim Data" staying in Saudi Arabia.

Add a section to your Guide (8.4):

Data Classification:

Level 1 (Public): Hotel names, general rates.

Level 3 (Sensitive/Personal): Pilgrim names, passport scans, payment records.

Requirement: Level 3 data MUST NOT leave Saudi borders. Ensure your S3-compatible storage (for passport uploads) is a Saudi-based bucket (e.g., STC Cloud or Oracle Jeddah Region).

4. Missing Packager & Config Files
You listed the "Files Reference," but you missed these critical production-ready files:

.dockerignore & Dockerfile: Essential for deploying to modern Saudi cloud providers (SDAIA or STC).

security.txt: A standard file placed in public/.well-known/ that tells Saudi white-hat hackers how to report a bug to you responsibly.

ecosystem.config.js (PM2): If you are using a VPS instead of Docker, you need this to ensure the server restarts automatically if it crashes.
