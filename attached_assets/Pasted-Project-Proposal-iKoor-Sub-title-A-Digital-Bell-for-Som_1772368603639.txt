Project Proposal: iKoor
Sub-title: A Digital "Bell" for Somalia’s Biodiversity & Environmental Resilience

Date: March 2026

Location: Federal Republic of Somalia

1. Executive Summary
Somalia is home to globally unique biodiversity that is currently undocumented in digital global databases. iKoor is a mobile-first, citizen-science platform designed to bridge this gap. By empowering local communities, students, and pastoralists to report wildlife and plant sightings, iKoor will create the first real-time, high-resolution map of Somalia’s natural heritage. This data will directly support the National Biodiversity Strategy and the 2026 Voluntary National Review (VNR) for the UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).

2. The Problem Statement
Despite having the longest coastline in mainland Africa and unique endemic species (like the Somali Wild Ass and Frankincense trees), Somalia faces:

Data Scarcity: A lack of localized, up-to-date biodiversity data for policy-making.

Security Risks: Poaching threats to endangered species due to unmanaged location data.

Climate Vulnerability: Rapid habitat loss from droughts and deforestation (charcoal trade) that goes unmonitored.

3. The Solution: iKoor
iKoor (named after the traditional Somali camel bell) serves as a "digital signal" for the environment.

Mobile Observation: Users capture photos/audio of species with GPS timestamps.

Offline First: Designed for the Somali interior; data saves locally and uploads when 4G/Wi-Fi is available.

AI & Community ID: Uses computer vision and a network of Somali biologists to verify sightings.

"Poacher-Proof" Privacy: Automatic "Geoprivacy" for endangered species, obscuring coordinates from the public while sharing them with verified conservationists.

4. Strategic Objectives
Digital Mapping: Document 50,000+ biodiversity observations across all 18 regions within 24 months.

Community Engagement: Train 500 "Environment Ambassadors" in universities (Mogadishu, Hargeisa, Garowe).

Policy Support: Provide the MoECC with a dashboard to monitor "Life on Land" (SDG 15) and "Life Below Water" (SDG 14).

Language Inclusion: Full Somali-language interface to ensure pastoralist participation.
